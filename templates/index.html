About Tomato Plant Diseases

Tomato plants are susceptible to various diseases such as blight, spot, and wilt. These diseases can significantly affect plant health and yield. Early detection is crucial for effective treatment and prevention.

Common Diseases

Blight

Blight causes dark spots on leaves and stems, leading to wilting and death of the plant. It's crucial to remove affected plants and apply fungicide.

Spot

Spot manifests as circular spots on leaves and fruit, which can lead to defoliation and reduced fruit quality. Management includes crop rotation and fungicides.

Wilt

Wilt is characterized by the yellowing and wilting of leaves, often caused by fungi or bacteria. Ensuring good soil drainage and removing infected plants helps control wilt.
